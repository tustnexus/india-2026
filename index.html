To Corporate Leaders:
We Need to Talk About 2026

We've built something remarkable. But the playbook that got us here won't be sufficient for what's ahead. Investments, innovation, and jobs have transformed our economy, but an uncomfortable truth awaits.

The Wake-Up Call

Remember the tech layoffs? Thousands of highly credentialed Indian engineers at Google, Microsoft, and Meta were let go. They had the degrees. They had the certifications. What they lacked was leverage.

When global markets optimize, areas without strategic positioning become cost centers to cut, not capabilities to protect.

"Credentials without leverage is just educated unemployment waiting to happen."

The Credential Paradox

Why high skills didn't save jobs in the absence of strategic leverage.

What Leverage Means Globally

Top economies don't just participate; they dominate specific verticals. We must move from "Cost Innovation" to "Strategic Indispensability".

The Race Against Time

Workforce growth vs. The "Labor Arbitrage" squeeze.

The Demographic Reality

We have the world's largest young workforce entering employment age. Unlike the outsourcing wave, today's graduates face AI displacement and reshoring.

Labor arbitrage is a race to the bottom. As skills get commoditized, we must build capacity in the premium skills zone.

Creating jobs isn't enough. We need to create leverage.

The 2026 Leverage Playbook

Strategic hard work scales to every business size.

Tier 1 10 Cr

Under 100 Employees

  • ✔ Build proprietary standards for India-specific challenges.
  • ✔ Partner with institutes to co-design curricula (upstream talent).
  • ✔ Export-First: Target Africa/SE Asia to build references & pricing power.
Tier 2 100 Cr

Under 1000 Employees

  • ✔ Train & finance 20-30 smaller vendors (Domestic Supply Chain).
  • ✔ R&D Consortiums: Pool resources with peers & IITs for Indian-owned tech.
  • ✔ Talent exchange programs vs. migration.
Tier 3 Listed

Large Cap / Conglomerates

  • ✔ Institution Building: Like Shiv Nadar and Jio Institutes or Foundation training.
  • ✔ Invest in capability building that outlasts business cycles.
  • ✔ Plan 10 years ahead.

Shareholder vs. Stakeholder

Leverage creates stakeholder improvement, one step at a time. Unlike EU peers who use state-backed safety nets to shift all at once, India must build incrementally.

  • 1 Build vendor ecosystems = Strengthen 30 businesses.
  • 2 Co-fund research = Create industry knowledge.
  • 3 Train talent = Build national capability.

"This is the VC model applied to national competitiveness."
